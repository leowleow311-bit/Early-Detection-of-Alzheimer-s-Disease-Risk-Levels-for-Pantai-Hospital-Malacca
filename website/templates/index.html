What is Alzheimer's Disease?


Alzheimer's disease is a progressive neurological disorder and the leading cause of dementia worldwide. It gradually impairs memory, thinking, and behavior, eventually affecting the ability to perform everyday tasks. Caused by abnormal protein buildup in the brain, Alzheimer’s primarily affects people over 65 but can also occur earlier. While there is no cure, treatments and lifestyle adjustments can help manage symptoms and improve quality of life.
